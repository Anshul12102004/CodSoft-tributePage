KRISHNAKUMAR

KUNNATH

Krishnakumar Kunnath, popularly known as KK, was born on August 23, 1968, in Delhi, India. He grew up with a deep love for music, which he pursued passionately from a young age. KK graduated from Kirori Mal College, Delhi University , where he was actively involved in music and participated in various competitions.

KK's career took off in the late 1990s, and he quickly became one of India's most beloved playback singers. His debut album, Pal, released in 1999, was a massive hit and included iconic songs like Pal and Yaaron. These tracks became anthems for friendship and nostalgia, earning KK a dedicated fan base.

KK's versatility as a singer allowed him to sing across various genres, including pop, rock, and classical. He worked with numerous leading composers and film directors in the Indian music industry. Some of his most memorable Bollywood songs include:

  • Aankhon Mein Teri from Om Shanti Om (2007)
  • Beete Lamhein from The Train(2007)
  • Khuda Jaane from Bachna Ae Haseeno (2008)
  • Abhi Abhi from Jo Hum Chahein (2012)
  • Kaise Bataaoon from 3G (2013)

KK's distinctive voice and emotive singing style made him a favorite among music lovers. He was known for his ability to convey deep emotions through his songs, making them resonate with listeners of all ages.

Tragically, KK passed away on May 31, 2022, after performing at a concert in Kolkata. His sudden demise was a significant loss to the music industry and his fans worldwide. KK's music continues to live on, touching hearts and inspiring new generations of music enthusiasts.
